About Me

Hi! I'm Kazibwe Francis, a passionate software engineer currently interning at DevelopersHub Corporation. I'm also the Secretary of the Makerere Tech Society and proudly serve as the Class Representative for Year 2 Software Engineering at Makerere University. With a deep love for technology and problem-solving, I thrive on developing innovative solutions that make an impact.

Right now, I'm working on FarmEase, a powerful and intuitive cow management application built with Flutter. Designed for modern farmers, this app simplifies cattle tracking, milk production monitoring, and feed management. With features like QR code scanning, AI-powered feed recommendations, and document management, FarmEase is transforming herd management for Ndejje University, helping farmers maintain accurate records and optimize productivity.

My journey into tech started at a young age, fueled by curiosity and a desire to build real-world solutions. Over time, I have honed my expertise in a diverse range of programming languages and frameworks, including:

  • Dart & Flutter: My go-to stack for building cross-platform mobile applications.
  • Firebase: Integrating real-time databases, authentication, and cloud functions to power scalable apps.
  • PHP & Laravel: Developing robust, scalable web applications with seamless backend integrations.

Beyond coding, I enjoy collaborating with fellow developers, contributing to open-source projects, and participating in hackathons. I'm always eager to explore new technologies, refine my problem-solving skills, and push the boundaries of what's possible in software development.

In my free time, you'll find me reading tech blogs, watching programming tutorials, and experimenting with the latest development tools. I'm particularly fascinated by data science and machine learning, and I’m always looking for ways to integrate AI into my projects.

Feel free to connect with me—whether it's for tech discussions, collaborations, or just sharing ideas. I'm always open to new opportunities and exciting challenges!
